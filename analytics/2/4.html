What do daily transactions represent?

Daily transactions measure the number of transactions processed by a blockchain each day. In this graph, the data is presented as a 7-day moving average, which smooths short-term fluctuations, and on a logarithmic scale. This metric is important because it reflects the intensity of network usage over time. A high number of daily transactions generally suggests that a blockchain is actively used. However, this indicator must be interpreted with caution because a large transaction count does not necessarily imply high-value economic activity. Some networks may generate large volumes through low-cost transfers or repetitive on-chain operations.

Distribution of transaction activity within the portfolio

The graph shows clear differences in transaction intensity across the cryptocurrencies included in the portfolio. TRON stands out clearly by maintaining the highest transaction volume throughout the period, with around 10 million transactions per day. This is very consistent with the first chart analysed (Active Addresses). However, as noted previously, a large proportion of TRON’s activity relates to stablecoin transfers and low-cost transactions, which may inflate its apparent usage without necessarily reflecting stronger economic fundamentals. A second group includes XRP and Ethereum, both of which show high transaction volumes, generally in the range of 1 to 4 million daily transactions. XRP appears to grow significantly toward the end of the period, whereas Ethereum remains relatively stable overall despite some fluctuations. Bitcoin occupies an intermediate position and shows relatively stable activity over time, which is not necessarily a sign of weakness, as it is often used for larger-value transactions and as a store of value rather than for high-frequency transfers. Finally, Litecoin, Dogecoin, Cardano and Bitcoin Cash remain at significantly lower levels.

Recommendations

From a portfolio perspective, the strategy should focus on prioritizing assets with sustained and diversified usage. Ethereum should remain a core holding due to its consistent activity and broad range of use cases. TRON, despite its high transaction volume, should not be overweighted, as its activity is concentrated in specific use cases and may not fully reflect long-term value. The recent increase in XRP’s activity makes it worth monitoring more closely, but any adjustment in allocation should be based on confirmation from other indicators. Assets with persistently low transaction levels should be reassessed or kept as minor positions, unless supported by strong fundamentals elsewhere. Overall, the portfolio should favor assets showing consistent and meaningful usage patterns while avoiding over-reliance on metrics that may not fully capture long-term value.

Conclusion

The analysis of daily transactions reveals a highly uneven pattern of blockchain usage across the portfolio. Some assets such as TRON, Ethereum and XRP show strong transaction activity, but this metric alone does not fully determine quality. High volumes may reflect real adoption, but they may also be driven by low-cost or highly concentrated forms of usage. As a result, daily transactions should be interpreted as a valuable indicator of network intensity, but not as a standalone measure of strength. For portfolio construction, the most attractive assets are those that combine sustained transaction activity with broader and more diversified fundamentals.
