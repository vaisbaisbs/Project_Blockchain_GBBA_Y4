Ethereum’s network activity

This graph analyzes Ethereum’s network activity through two key indicators: transaction volume and gas usage. While transactions measure the number of operations executed on the network, gas reflects the computational cost required to process them. Together, these two metrics provide insight into both the level of activity and the intensity of resource usage on the network. Over the observed period, transaction volume shows noticeable fluctuations with several short-term increases and declines, reflecting real-time changes in user activity. In contrast, gas usage, appearing relatively smoother, follows a similar overall pattern. In fact, both metrics tend to move together: periods of increased transaction activity are generally associated with higher gas usage. This relationship highlights Ethereum’s role as a platform supporting complex interactions, where variations in user activity directly impact the resources required to process transactions. At the same time, it illustrates how the network adjusts dynamically to changes in demand.

Recommendations

Based on this analysis, Ethereum should remain a core component of the portfolio due to its strong and sustained level of activity. However, it is important to consider not only the level of usage, but also the efficiency of the network. Periods of increased activity can lead to higher transaction costs, potentially affecting accessibility and user experience. For this reason, exposure to Ethereum should be maintained while closely monitoring its performance during periods of high demand. This approach allows the portfolio to benefit from its strengths while managing potential efficiency constraints.

Conclusion

The analysis of Ethereum’s network activity highlights its importance as one of the most active and widely used blockchains in the portfolio. The observed correlation between transaction volume and gas usage confirms that increases in user activity are directly linked to higher demand for computational resources. At the same time, it underlines the challenges associated with managing this demand efficiently. Therefore, Ethereum represents a high-value and essential asset, whose long-term performance will depend on its ability to sustain growth while improving scalability and reducing network constraints.
