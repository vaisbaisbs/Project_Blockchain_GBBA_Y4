What is the Sortino Ratio?

The Sortino Ratio measures how well an asset performs while focusing only on downside risk. It helps us understand if price increases are stable or if they come with strong losses. A positive value means that prices are rising in a relatively stable way, while a negative value means that prices are falling or unstable. A bull market refers to a period where prices increase over time. A bear market is a period where prices decline. A rally is a short-term strong increase in prices.

Overall Price Behavior

The graph shows that crypto prices move in cycles, alternating between bull and bear phases.Most cryptocurrencies follow the same direction, which means the market moves together. However, their intensity is different. Bitcoin and Ethereum show smoother price movements, while altcoins such as ADA, TRX, and XLM show stronger rallies but also deeper drops.

Price Evolution and Market Phases

In 2022, the market clearly enters a bear market. Prices fall and become very unstable, which explains the negative Sortino Ratio. This is linked to major events such as the war in Ukraine, high inflation, and strong interest rate increases.

This result is consistent with findings from Impact of Russia-Ukraine war attention on cryptocurrency.

At the same time, the Terra Luna collapse (a failed stablecoin system) and the FTX collapse (a major exchange failure) created panic. Prices dropped sharply across the entire market. In 2023, the market begins to stabilize. This period marks the end of the bear phase and the beginning of a slow recovery. Prices become less volatile, and the Sortino Ratio improves as downside risk decreases.

In early 2024, the market enters a bull market. Prices increase more steadily, supported by the approval of Bitcoin ETFs and the arrival of institutional investors. This leads to more stable growth. Between July and October 2024, a correction occurs. This is a normal phase inside a bull market. After strong price increases, investors take profits, and uncertainty about central bank decisions leads to a temporary decline. This reduces the Sortino Ratio.

In 2025, the market becomes more speculative. Strong rallies appear, especially for altcoins like TRX, ADA, and XLM. These rapid increases are driven by investor enthusiasm, new technologies, and leverage. However, these rallies are often followed by sharp corrections, which explains the strong fluctuations in the Sortino Ratio.

In 2026, the market shows signs of a new bearish phase. Prices become less stable again, possibly due to tighter financial conditions and regulatory uncertainty. Downside risk increases.

Portfolio Health

The Sortino Ratio shows that the portfolio has an unstable health profile. It performs well during bullish periods, with relatively controlled downside risk, but becomes fragile during market downturns. Negative values observed during crisis periods indicate that losses are significant when the market declines. This means that the portfolio is highly dependent on favorable market conditions and struggles to maintain stability in uncertain environments.

Conclusion

The Sortino Ratio analysis shows that the 10-crypto portfolio moves mostly together, following the same market trends. However, not all cryptocurrencies behave the same way. Bitcoin and Ethereum are more stable and have fewer large losses. On the other hand, altcoins like ADA, TRX, and XLM can grow faster during bull markets but also fall more strongly during bad periods. This means that some cryptocurrencies make the portfolio more stable, while others increase both potential returns and risk. Overall, the portfolio can perform well when the market is rising, but it remains sensitive to downturns, with higher downside risk during bear markets.
