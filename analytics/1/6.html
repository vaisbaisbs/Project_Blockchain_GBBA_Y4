What is the Monte Carlo Simulation?

The Monte Carlo simulation is used to model the possible future evolution of the crypto portfolio by generating many random scenarios. Each blue line represents a possible path the portfolio could take over time. The yellow line shows the average performance, while the green and red lines represent the best and worst scenarios. This method helps visualize uncertainty and understand how the portfolio might behave under different market conditions.

Overall Portfolio Behavior

All simulations start from the same initial value, but quickly diverge as time progresses. Some scenarios show strong growth, while others show a clear decline. The average line remains relatively stable, which suggests moderate expected performance. However, the large spread between scenarios highlights that actual outcomes can differ significantly. This shows that the portfolio does not follow a single predictable path.

Risk

The increasing gap between the minimum and maximum scenarios reflects a high level of uncertainty. On one side, the portfolio can reach strong gains, as shown by the upper curve. On the other side, it can also experience noticeable losses, as shown by the lower curve. This means that even if the expected return is stable, the risk around it is high.

Future Outlook and Recommendations

The Monte Carlo simulation shows that the portfolio has strong return potential but remains highly uncertain. The wide gap between scenarios shows that future performance can vary significantly depending on market conditions. It is important to note that the current portfolio composition is generated by an optimization model that maximizes the Sharpe Ratio based on historical data. This means that the weights are not chosen manually, but selected because they performed well in the past. However, this also creates some limitations. The model tends to favor more volatile altcoins such as TRX and XRP, as they offer higher returns, even if they increase overall risk. As a result, the portfolio becomes more exposed to strong market fluctuations. To improve stability, it would be better to slightly reduce exposure to these assets and increase the weight of more stable cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum. The Sortino Ratio and volatility analysis also show that the portfolio is very sensitive to market downturns. During bearish periods, losses can be significant because most cryptocurrencies move in the same direction. This means that diversification within crypto is limited. A better balance between stable assets and higher-growth cryptocurrencies would help improve the overall risk-return profile. It could also be useful to adapt the strategy over time. During highly speculative periods, reducing exposure to riskier assets can help secure gains, while increasing positions during recovery phases can support growth. Finally, future market conditions may remain uncertain, especially due to geopolitical tensions. Events such as international conflicts can increase volatility and lead to sudden price movements in cryptocurrencies. This makes diversification and risk management even more important. Overall, the portfolio should aim to reduce extreme volatility while maintaining good long-term return potential.
