What does this graph show?

This graph shows how much crypto prices move over time. Volatility measures the intensity of price changes.High volatility means large and rapid price movements, while low volatility means more stable prices.

Overall Price Behavior

The graph confirms that crypto markets are highly unstable. Prices go through phases of calm followed by strong movements. Bitcoin and Ethereum are relatively more stable, while altcoins such as TRX, ADA, and XLM show stronger volatility. This means that altcoins react more strongly during both rallies and market drops.

Price Evolution and Market Phases

In 2022, volatility is very high across all assets. This corresponds to a bear market, where prices fall and move unpredictably. This instability is caused by global uncertainty, including the war in Ukraine, inflation, and rising interest rates. The situation is made worse by the Terra Luna and FTX collapses, which trigger panic and large price swings.

In 2023, volatility decreases as the market stabilizes. Prices move more slowly, showing the end of the bear phase and the beginning of recovery.

In 2024, volatility remains present but becomes more controlled during the bull market. Prices increase more steadily thanks to institutional investment and ETF approvals. However, during mid-2024, volatility rises again due to a correction phase, which is typical after strong price growth.

In early 2025, volatility increases again, especially for altcoins. This period is marked by strong rallies, driven by speculation and investor enthusiasm. Prices rise quickly but also fall sharply, which explains the high volatility.

In 2026, volatility rises again, suggesting a return to a more uncertain and possibly bearish environment. Prices become less predictable due to macroeconomic pressure and regulation.

Why Are Cryptocurrencies Generally So Volatile?

Portfolio Health

The volatility analysis highlights that the portfolio is exposed to a high level of risk. Frequent spikes in volatility, especially during crisis periods, show that price movements can become unstable very quickly. Although some assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum help reduce extreme fluctuations, the presence of more volatile altcoins significantly increases overall risk. This suggests that the portfolio is not stable and remains sensitive to sudden market changes.

Conclusion

The volatility analysis shows that the 10-crypto portfolio is highly exposed to strong price movements, especially during periods of market stress or speculation.Although all assets react to the same global environment, their level of volatility differs significantly. Bitcoin and Ethereum show more controlled price movements, while altcoins such as TRX, ADA, and XLM contribute most to the portfolio’s volatility. This means that the overall risk of the portfolio is mainly driven by smaller and more speculative cryptocurrencies. The portfolio offers high return potential, but this comes with a high level of instability. During bullish phases, volatility can create strong opportunities, but during downturns, it can lead to rapid losses.
