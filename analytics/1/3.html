What is the Optimal Portfolio Composition?

This chart shows the composition of the optimal crypto portfolio identified using the efficient frontier. Each percentage represents the weight of a cryptocurrency in the portfolio that maximizes the Sharpe Ratio. The Sharpe Ratio is a measure used to evaluate how much return a portfolio generates compared to the level of risk taken. A higher Sharpe Ratio means better performance for the same level of risk. The goal of this portfolio is to find a good balance between return and risk. Here, the portfolio reaches a return of 34.54%, a volatility of 57.60%, and a Sharpe Ratio of 0.60. This suggests that the portfolio can generate relatively high returns, but with a fairly high level of risk.

Overall Price Behavior

The portfolio is not equally distributed and is clearly concentrated in a few main assets. TRX and XRP represent a large part of the allocation, followed by ETC and BCH. On the other hand, Bitcoin and Ethereum have smaller weights, while other cryptocurrencies like ADA, XLM, LTC, and DASH play only a minor role. This shows that the portfolio is mainly driven by altcoins rather than by Bitcoin and Ethereum, which is quite interesting given their usual importance in the market.

Risk

This composition reflects a trade-off between stability and performance. Bitcoin and Ethereum are included to bring some stability, while altcoins such as TRX, XRP, and ETC are used to increase expected returns. However, these altcoins are also more volatile, which increases the overall risk of the portfolio. This explains why the volatility remains relatively high. In simple terms, Bitcoin and Ethereum help stabilize the portfolio, while altcoins improve performance but also make the portfolio more risky.

Diversification

Even though the portfolio includes 10 cryptocurrencies, it is still concentrated in a few main assets. This means that most of the performance comes from a small number of cryptocurrencies, while the others have only a limited impact. At the same time, many cryptocurrencies tend to move in the same direction. Because of this, holding several assets does not significantly reduce risk. The portfolio is still largely affected by overall market movements.

Price Evolution and Market Phases

The strong presence of altcoins can be linked to how the market behaved in recent years. During bullish periods, especially in 2024 and 2025, altcoins tend to grow faster than Bitcoin and Ethereum because of higher investor interest and speculation. But during market downturns, these same assets also fall more sharply, which increases the risk of the portfolio. This explains why the portfolio performs well in good market conditions but becomes more fragile when the market declines.

Interpretation

This portfolio represents the mathematically optimal allocation based on the efficient frontier, meaning it maximizes the Sharpe Ratio given past data. However, this result should be interpreted with caution. The model favors assets that offer higher returns, even if they are more volatile, which explains the strong concentration in altcoins such as TRX and XRP. In practice, such a concentration may increase the portfolio’s exposure to sudden market downturns, especially during periods of high volatility or uncertainty. Therefore, while the portfolio is optimal from a theoretical perspective, a more balanced allocation could be considered in real-world conditions to improve stability and reduce risk.

Conclusion

The optimal portfolio composition shows that the best-performing portfolio is not the most diversified, but the one that balances risk and return effectively. Bitcoin and Ethereum help bring stability, but most of the performance comes from altcoins like TRX and XRP. This confirms that in the crypto market, higher returns are often linked to higher risk. Overall, the portfolio can perform well when the market is rising, but it remains exposed to strong downside risk during negative periods.
